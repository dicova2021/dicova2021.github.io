Diagnosing COVID-19 using Acoustics

Special Session in Interspeech 2021

About

The COVID-19 pandemic has resulted in more than 40 million infections, and more than 1.5 million casualties. The global crisis spans across 200 countries. Large scale testing, social distancing, and face masks have been critical measures to help contain the spread of the infection. While the list of symptoms is regular updated, it is established that COVID-19 seriously impairs normal functioning of the respiratory system. Does this alter the acoustic characteristics of breathe, cough, and speech sounds produced through the respiratory system? This is an open question. By participating in this special session at Interspeech 2021 you will be taking a dive to find some answers. A COVID-19 diagnosis methodology based on acoustic signal analysis can provide a remote, sclable, and economical means for testing of individuals. This can supplement the existing nucleotides based COVID-19 testing methods, such as RT-PCR and RAT. The timeliness, global societal importance of the problem warrants focussed effort from researchers across the globe, including from the fields of medical and respiratory sciences, mathematical sciences, and machine learning engineers.
